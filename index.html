The History of Angel Island

Angel Island, located in San Francisco Bay, is a historic site that served as the primary immigration station for approximately 175,000 Chinese and 60,000 Japanese immigrants from 1910 to 1940 and a naval base during World War I. The islandâ€™s facilities were slated for demolition in the 1970s but were preserved, and nowadays the island hosts hiking trails, campsites, and a visitor center.

Angel Island is the west-coast counterpart of the more well-known Ellis Island in New York, with the most significant difference being that Angel Island primarily handled immigration from Asia while Ellis Island handled European immigrants.
